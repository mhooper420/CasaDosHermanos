A home designed for modern living on timeless principles. "Heirloom" means built to last and to deepen with use — never traditional, inherited, or ornamental. Calmness over excitement, permanence over novelty, material quality over visual complexity.

§05 · Systems

One backbone, three households

A UniFi prosumer backbone, sized for three semi-independent households sharing one connection — which is why VLAN segmentation, not just coverage, is the design driver. The pre-drywall window is the only practical one; all cabling goes through KB's Structured Wiring option. AP mix locked at 3× U7 Pro.

Smart-home MVP · technology should disappear

Build the irreversible layer now; keep the visible layer small, swappable, ecosystem-light. Brain: Home Assistant, self-hosted at the head-end (local, no subscription); Matter-over-Thread + Zigbee for device longevity.

§09 · Perceived-custom strategy

Built-In Look, Bolt-On Budget

Replicating millwork without millwork money

True custom millwork is off the table — the look is not. Built-ins read as custom because of a handful of visual signals, not the joinery underneath. Buy the signals, skip the joinery: hit the six tells below and a low-cost carcass reads as bespoke at three paces. The single highest-leverage move is free — paint the cabinet and the wall the same colour so the box dissolves into architecture.

The six tells of “custom” — and the budget fix for each

Signal the eye reads as customThe expensive wayThe bolt-on-budget fix
Full-height, floor-to-ceilingBuilt to sizeStack stock frame-cabinets + a painted filler & crown to bridge to the ceiling
No gaps to wall / ceilingScribed custom panelsScribe-cut filler strips; caulk the final reveal
Sits “grown from” the floorCustom baseBox out or remove the toe-kick; run the room’s baseboard across the face
Cabinet disappears into the wallSame custom finishPaint wall + cabinet the same colour & sheen — the highest-leverage move, near-free
Tied into the houseOriginal trimCarry the existing crown + baseboard across the unit
Quality “feel”Solid hardwoodSpend the saved money on hardware + integrated LED — tiny budget, huge perceived lift

The cost ladder — cheapest to priciest, all reversible-capable

TierMethodBest for
1 · Paint & trim illusionMDF battens / stop-moulding on painted drywall — slat walls, board-and-batten, shaker panels. Cost is mostly paint + cheap moulding.Primary navy slat wall · powder · entry · stair
2 · Floating & framedThick-front floating shelves, picture rails, a single cabinet run framed with trim.Lounge · loft · secondary walls
3 · Frame-cabinet semi-customKitchen-grade IKEA-type carcasses + upgraded/painted fronts + the six fixes above.Office bookcase wall · kitchen · coffee-bar run
4 · Targeted true customOne hero element only, where the saved budget concentrates.Pick one: e.g. the kitchen island face

Spend where it counts

  • Spend on guest-facing, eye-level, high-dwell surfaces: kitchen island faces, office bookcase fronts, powder.
  • Save on private / low-dwell / behind-a-door surfaces, cabinet interiors, and Daniel’s separately-funded basement (cheapest methods only).
  • Hardware is the cheapest tell to fix and the loudest if wrong — never builder-grade on a hero face.

The tells to avoid — what screams “cheap”

  • Visible gaps / reveals to wall or ceiling · exposed stock toe-kick · sheen mismatch between cabinet and wall paint.
  • Builder-grade hardware · non-full-height boxes with a dead gap above · visible fasteners or unfinished edges.

Cost reality

Unknown · needs verification As a rule of thumb, true custom millwork commonly runs several times the cost of a frame-cabinet hack — treat that as a heuristic, not a quote; prices vary by Denver-metro labour, year, and finish. Check path: get one cabinetmaker quote for the office wall, price the frame-box + fronts + trim equivalent, and compare — the delta sets the budget and your confidence. Named products (IKEA frame systems, third-party fronts) are examples; verify current US/region availability & pricing.

Reversibility holds. Every Tier-1/2 method and “freestanding-styled-as-built-in” keeps shared / transition spaces (loft) reversible — consistent with the Occupancy rule.
